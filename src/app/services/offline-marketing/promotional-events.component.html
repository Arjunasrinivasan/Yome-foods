Promotional Events

Promotional Events at Local Market

Did you ever attend an event that has sponsors and there are banners all across the event venue, which probably says ‘powered by...’? Yes, those are promotional events which are paid by the company, which helps them to reach a specific business goal. Restaurants normally do this type of event to create goodwill and to give a taste of their cuisine to potential new customers.

Restaurants sponsor events that their customers relate to, if there is some festive season going on, they will bring some dish specifically for that time period, in this way the customer feels that the restaurant cares about the same thing and creates a positive feeling about the brand. But the sole purpose of these promotional events is not the customer’s feeling because it also creates awareness about the brand. Apart from the awareness, the purpose of these promotional events is to increase the audience to reach and drive more sales.

A promotional event is an excellent way to get more media coverage which increases the visibility of the brand. It also opens new doors for your brand, as you may stumble upon other businesses and VIP members for collaboration.

Tie-up with local Shops/Supermarket/Stores

We normally do grocery shopping from our local shops, but have you ever come across a local shop which gives you a referral code or voucher on order value of a certain amount? These types of local shops have tie-up with restaurants that want to create awareness in that particular area. In this way, the restaurants can rest assure that it will get new customer and the local shop will keep selling higher amount of grocery. You can do this when you are a new brand or you are starting with a new dish. In this way, you can gauge if the new cuisine is going to be a success or not and you are not even spending more on advertising.
