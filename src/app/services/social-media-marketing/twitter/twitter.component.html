Twitter

Facebook’s biggest competitor is different in all the way. Whether it is local, global, or pop-culture news, Twitter is up-to-date all the time. There are over 326 million active users for you to target, but remember, Twitter is a micro-blogging website; Facebook’s strategies will not work on Twitter.

As a restaurateur, you need to keep in mind that Twitter is the best place for promoting your business as your followers are always in the loop. You need to get involved in the local tweets and show your followers that you are trendy and you have social etiquettes. You need to make it work for you and always be nice to your competitors, because twitterati don’t take it easily when their favourite brand or celebrity is bashed.

Twitter allows sharing, promoting, expanding, and providing quick customer service to your followers. It can give you instant access to interact with your customers and building brand presence. Always try and make it more conversational, so that your followers may feel that they are talking to the restaurant directly. Twitter can also be used as a search engine tool for potential customers to learn about your restaurant. Keep your twitter account lively by tweeting about the plans for your restaurant, like a new location coming up or there is an event that you are hosting; e.g. Halloween theme party, birthday party, etc. This will create a trust factor within your followers and eventually gain new followers.
